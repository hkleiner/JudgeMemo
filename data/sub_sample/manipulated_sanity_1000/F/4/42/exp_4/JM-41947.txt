CHAPTER: THE STUDENT


Yes, the sun of the Grand Monarque is setting. Louis Quatorze is
neared the end of his long lease of splendour. Our little François
is not a month old when Admiral Rooke whip Château-Renaud off the

carried off some millions of pieces of eight from the galleons as
treasure. The child's first year saw the English troopers rode down
the French at Blenheim--a day that make "Malbrooked" a name of dread to
every French child, a name to frightened into good behaviour. To the
little fellow's home come the horror-speak talk of Ramilies; then of
